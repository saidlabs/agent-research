# SAID Labs — Agent Research

**Open research on autonomous AI agent infrastructure.**

We're exploring the frontiers of what autonomous AI agents can do: self-directed research, multi-agent coordination, on-chain identity, reputation systems, cross-chain communication, agentic economies, and emergent agent behavior.

## Mission

Advance the entire field of autonomous AI agent infrastructure — not just one protocol or platform. Think **DeepMind for agents**: rigorous, curious, opinionated, and building in public.

## Research Areas

### 🔗 Agent-to-Agent (A2A) Protocols
- Comparative analysis: Google A2A, MCP (Model Context Protocol), ACP, SAID Protocol
- Latency, reliability, and interoperability benchmarks
- Cross-chain messaging patterns

### 🆔 On-Chain Identity
- ERC-8004, SAID Protocol, DID (Decentralized Identifiers), Verifiable Credentials
- What does "portable agent identity" actually require?
- How does identity verification scale across chains?

### 🤝 Multi-Agent Coordination
- Discovery mechanisms: how agents find each other
- Trust and reputation: how agents decide who to work with
- Coordination patterns: swarms, markets, hierarchies

### 🧠 Autonomous Decision-Making
- When should agents act independently vs. ask for approval?
- Risk assessment and bounded autonomy
- Learning from outcomes

### 💰 Agentic Economies
- x402 micropayments: actual usage patterns
- Agent-to-agent commerce: what works, what doesn't
- Economic incentives for coordination

## Methodology

We follow the **Karpathy Research Loop**:

1. **IDENTIFY** — What specific question needs answering?
2. **DESIGN** — How will we find the answer? What sources?
3. **EXECUTE** — Search, read, analyze, collect data
4. **ANALYZE** — What does the evidence say? Confidence level?
5. **ITERATE** — If confidence is low, refine and search again
6. **REPORT** — Present findings with evidence and confidence

**No guessing. No hype. Evidence-based only.**
